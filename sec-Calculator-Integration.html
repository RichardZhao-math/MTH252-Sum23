    Section 5.2 Does calculators evaluate integrals "smartly"?

    There are some computer algebra system (CAS), including WolframAlpha
     1 
    www.wolframalpha.com/calculators/integral-calculator
    , Symbolab
     2 
    www.symbolab.com/solver/integral-calculator
    , Integral Calculator
     3 
    www.integral-calculator.com/
    , and so many others, to evaluate integrals.
    🔗
    As we saw in the previous task, there are multiple approaches to evaluate integrals, some of which may be easier than others. We know CAS is a pretty powerful tool to evaluate integrals, but does it always evaluate integrals using the most "efficient" way?
    🔗
    In this activity, you will compete with CAS to evaluate the following integral and see who will give the most elegant solution.
    \begin{equation*} \int \left(\sin x + \cos x\right)^2 \, dx \end{equation*}
    🔗

    Exercises Part (a): Evaluation by hand

    Evaluate the integral by hand. Show your work by writing down each of the step AND explain a bit the rationale behind the steps.
    🔗
    Hint.
    We can’t split up the integral because of the square. Can we first simplify the integrant a bit?
    🔗
    Notice that we are working with trigonometric functions, and we know a lot of trigonometric identities that may be helpful to simplify the integrand...
    🔗
    🔗
    Solution.
    The key identities to use here are the pythagorean identity
    \begin{equation*} \sin^2(x) + \cos^2(x) = 1 \end{equation*}
    and the double-angle identity
    \begin{equation*} \sin(2x) = 2\sin(x)\cos(x) \end{equation*}
    Now let us start to evaluate this integral by hand!
    \begin{align*} \int \left(\sin x + \cos x\right)^2 \, dx \amp = \int\left(\sin^2(x) + 2\sin(x)\cos(x) + \cos^2(x)\right)\, dx \\ \amp = \int\left(1 + 2\sin(x)\cos(x)\right)\, dx \\ \amp = \int \left(1 + \sin(2x)\right)\, dx\\ \amp = x + \frac{1}{2}\cos(2x) + C \end{align*}
    🔗
    🔗
    🔗

    Exercises Part (b): Evaluation by CAS

    Go to Integral Calculator
     4 
    www.integral-calculator.com/
    and input "(sinx+cosx)^2". Then click Go!
    🔗
    After the CAS evaluated the integral, scroll down on the Result and click "Show steps" to see how the CAS evaluated the integral.
    🔗
    Your task here is to briefly summarize the approach the CAS used to evaluate this integral (don’t copy down the work exactly. Instead, summarize the process by saying what they did in each step).
    🔗
    Solution.
    Well what CAS did was to expand \((\sin x + \cos x)^2\) and broke it down using linearity as follows:
    \begin{align*} \int \left(\sin x + \cos x\right)^2 \amp = \int \left(\sin^2 x + 2\sin(x)\cos(x) + \cos^2(x)\right)\, dx \\ \amp = \int\sin^2(x)\, dx + \int 2\sin(x)\cos(x)\, dx + \int \cos^2(x)\, dx \end{align*}
    Well the middle integral is easy to evaluate but CAS uses the reduction formula for \(\int \sin^2(x)\, dx\) and \(\int \cos^2(x)\, dx\text{,}\) making the steps a lot more complicated.
    🔗
    🔗
