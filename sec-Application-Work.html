    Section 3.3 A Physics and Engineering Application -- Work

    You may have heard of a measurement called work before, as it measures the transfer of energy by a force acting on an object as it is displaced. In a nutshell, the work (\(W\)) can be computed by multiplying the force (\(F\)) with the displacement (\(d\)) together, like this
    \begin{equation*} W = F\, d \end{equation*}
    Notice that we can obtain the work if we know the (constant) force applied to an object and the displacement.

So the above formula is really limited in the sense that we donâ€™t know exactly what the value of \(F\) is (since it varies). Thankfully we know calculus now and calculus will come to our rescue (calculus is a study that deals with changes)!
